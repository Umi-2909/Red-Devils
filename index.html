BIOGRAPHY

Erik ten Hag was confirmed as Manchester United manager in April 2022, replacing Ralf Rangnick after the 2021/22 Premier League campaign.

Ten Hag had a 13-year playing career, including three spells with Twente, where he retired in 2002.

After his playing career finished, he was appointed as manager of Go Ahead Eagles in 2012, leading the team to their first promotion in 17 years during his only season at the club.

He moved to Bayern Munich's reserve team in June 2013 and two years later, was appointed the sporting director and head coach of Utrecht, where he won the manager of the year award in 2016.

The 52-year-old took over as Ajax head coach midway through the 2017/18 campaign and would lead them to the UEFA Champions League semi-final the following season.

That year, Ten Hag also claimed his first trophies as a manager, winning the league and cup double.

In January 2022, he became the fastest manager to reach 100 league wins with Ajax, achieving the feat in just 128 matches.

TROPHY ROOM

Manchester United are one of the most successful clubs in world football, with a glittering history of success on the field.

First Division/Premier League

On 22 April 2013, Manchester United won their 13th Premier League title and 20th English title overall by defeating Aston Villa 3–0 at Old Trafford.
During the season, Robin Van Persie won the title of Top Scorer with 26 goals. It is also the last season of the 26-year tenure of legendary coach Sir Alex Ferguson.

UEFA Champions League

UEFA Champions League final between Chelsea FC vs Manchester United FC took place on 21 May 2008 at the Luzhniki Stadium in Moscow, Russia.
Manchester United won 6–5 on penalties, after drawing 1-1 after extra time.
Edwin van der Sar then saved Nicolas Anelka's effort from Chelsea's seventh kick to secure Manchester United's third European Cup title.

UEFA Europa League

The Europan Final between AFC Ajax vs Manchester United FC took place on 24 May 2017 at Friends Arena in Solna, Stockholm, Sweden.
Manchester United won 2–0 to claim their first league title.
With this victory, Manchester United won all three major European trophies.

FA Cup

The FA Cup Final between Crystal Palace FC vs Manchester United FC on 21 May 2016 at Wembley Stadium.
The official match time ended with a score of 1-1, the final had to go into extra time. Jesse Lingard's long-range shot sent the ball into the top corner of Crystal Palace's goal, helping Manchester United win 2-1 and win the FA Cup for the 12th time.

League Cup

On February 26, 2023. The EFL Cup Final was played between Manchester United FC and Newcastle United FC at Wembley Stadium.
Manchester United defeat Newcastle United 2–0 with goals from Casemiro and Marcus Rashford in the first half to win their sixth title in the competition.

FIFA Club World Cup

FIFA Club World Cup an association football tournament for the champion clubs from each of FIFA's six continental confederations. The final match took place at the International Stadium Yokohama, Japan, on 21 December 2008.
Match between Ecuador's LDU Quito FC, CONMEBOL champions vs Manchester United FC, UEFA champions. Despite being 10 men behind at the start of the second half, Manchester United won 1–0 thanks to Wayne Rooney's 73rd minute goal.

Community Shield

2016 FA Community Shield was the 94th FA Community Shield. The match was contested by 2015–16 FA Cup winners Manchester United FC, and Leicester City FC, champions of the 2015–16 Premier League.
It was held at Wembley Stadium a week before the Premier League season kicked off. Manchester United won the match 2–1 with goals from Jesse Lingard and Zlatan Ibrahimović.
